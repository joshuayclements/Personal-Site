ICHOR

Corporate Identity

Founded in 1857, on the island of Lesvos, Greece, Ichor Distilleries is one of the premiere manufactures of traditional Greek ouzo. The company has been owned and operated by the Hallas family since its beginning, and follows the families ouzo recipe that is said to have been handed down from generation to generation since first being created by monks in the 14th century on Mt. Athos. The companies name, Ichor, was derived from the ethereal golden liquid that was said to be possessed only by the immortals and gods of Greek Mythology. Its unique flavor and characteristics stem from the Star Anise and other local plants found in the Agean.

As the company prepared to make its debut on the United States market place, Ichor was in need of a re-branding campaign that would appeal to American consumers. Following the companies brand attributes of Greek, celebratory, genteel, aromatic, and unique, a strategy with the goal of generating interest from their target demographic (of people ranging from ages 32 to 65) was put into place and that covered the areas of web, print, and product design. The new components of the brand that were created for this project included a logo, color palette, packaging, stationary suite, website, and labels.
